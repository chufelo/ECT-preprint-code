Excluded probes and reasons

  • BAO (DESI 2024): simplified methodology (Ω_m fixed, diagonal covariance, shape-only). Raw best-fit −0.013 is likely an artefact of fixing Ω_m independently. Proper analysis would require coupled (Ω_m, r_d, ε) likelihood with DESI 12×12 covariance matrix. Not used in headline — moved to "methodology-limited" category.
  • A_lens CMB lensing: linearized proxy A_lens ≈ 1 + κ_A·ε without full CAMB/CLASS Boltzmann calculation. Uses Planck observational σ (0.03) but omits theoretical systematic on linearization coefficient κ_A (typically 50-100% uncertainty for rough proxy). Narrow 1σ shown earlier was misleading. Would need proper Boltzmann treatment.
  • S_8 weak lensing (KiDS-1000): raw 1σ entirely below zero. Measures the S_8 tension, which ECT's ε > 0 would worsen, not resolve. Outside ECT's ε scope.
  • σ_8 cluster counts: same structural issue as S_8.
  • SN Ia: was mock data; permanently removed.
  • Age t₀: self-consistency only (uses H₀(ε) from Hubble). Text note.

Physics interpretation

  • Strong finding: five robust probes (Hubble+r_s, JWST, CC, fσ_8, ISW) are jointly consistent with uniform-ε at ε ≈ 0.032 (1σ width 0.007) under ECT's physical prior ε ≥ 0.
  • Tier A probes (Hubble, JWST) require ε > 0 → primary evidence for non-zero drift.
  • Tier B probes (CC, fσ_8, ISW) give upper bounds; the tightest (fσ_8 at ε < 0.036) happens to align just above Hubble's best-fit (0.032), producing a narrow joint window.
  • Preprint benchmark (ε ≈ 0.010) is below the joint 1σ region. The benchmark was chosen for conservative partial Hubble resolution, but the data actually prefers ε ~ 0.03 under uniform-ε. This suggests either: (a) benchmark should be revised to ~0.03, or (b) uniform-ε is inadequate and ECT's natural prediction is epoch-dependent ε(z) with higher values at early times.
