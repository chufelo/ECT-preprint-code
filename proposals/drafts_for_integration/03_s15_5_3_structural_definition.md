### §15.5.3 Joint effective allowed band and working benchmark

**[Opening bridge.]** With the five effective channels specified in §15.5.2, we now report the resulting joint effective allowed band, identify the working benchmark used throughout §15.5 and in the rest of the paper, and state its relationship to the earlier conservative illustrative point used in the previous preprint treatment of the Hubble mechanism (§\ref{sec:universe_age}, §\ref{sec:mp_hubble}).

**Headline result.** Under the retained five-probe set, adopting the uniform-ε effective ansatz of §15.5.1 and the physical prior ε ≥ 0, the intersection of the 1σ intervals of the two Primary channels with the 1σ upper bounds of the three Consistency channels admits a jointly allowed effective drift parameter in the narrow band

> ε ∈ [+0.029, +0.036]  (1σ),  ε ∈ [+0.021, +0.042]  (2σ),

centred near the midpoint ε ≈ 0.032. The 1σ region is bounded below by the JWST channel (lower edge ε = 0.029, eq. eps_jwst_retained) and bounded above by the fσ_8 channel (upper edge ε = 0.036, eq. eps_fsigma8_retained).

**No-prediction caveat.** This retained-five-probe effective band is an empirical result of the present diagnostic analysis, not a first-principles ECT prediction. It does not represent a measurement of a fundamental parameter of the theory, nor a direct reconstruction of an epoch-dependent ε(z). It is an effective consistency window under the uniform-ε ansatz, the ΛCDM-background proxy, and the retention/likelihood choices of §15.5.2.

**Why the band is nevertheless informative.** Three qualitative observations make the band a non-trivial consistency check rather than a circular restatement of the retention choice.
- *Distinct systematics.* The five retained channels have qualitatively distinct observational systematics, from CMB-era distance-ratio proxies to low-z RSD growth likelihoods. A narrow common admissible region under a single uniform-ε ansatz is not guaranteed a priori.
- *Two-sided primaries.* The two Primary channels (Hubble + r_s and JWST early-galaxy excess) provide two-sided effective extraction intervals whose 1σ intersection alone already defines a narrow positive-ε region compatible with the Consistency upper bounds; the band is not generated by the ε ≥ 0 prior alone.
- *Narrow width.* The 1σ width, Δε ≈ 0.007, is comparable to the smallest single-probe 1σ intervals (Hubble: 0.011; fσ_8 upper edge: 0.036), and is therefore not dominated by the broadest channel.

**Working benchmark (Benchmark B).** The working cosmological benchmark of the present effective analysis is the retained-five-probe effective band itself; we refer to it as Benchmark B, and equivalently as the *retained five-probe effective band* or the *working uniform-ε band*. Whenever a single-number illustration is needed in §15.5 or elsewhere in the paper (figure captions, single-row table entries), the phrasing used is contextual — *"using the midpoint of the retained band (ε ≈ 0.032) for illustration"* — rather than any standalone named constant; the midpoint is a presentation device, not a second benchmark entity.

**Parameter scaling under the working benchmark.** Under the uniform-ε ansatz evaluated at the band midpoint, the scaling of the relevant quantities across cosmic history is

| Quantity | z = 0 | z = 10 | z = 1100 |
|----------|:-----:|:------:|:--------:|
| (1+z)^{midpoint} | 1.000 | 1.078 | 1.241 |
| (1+z)^{2·midpoint} | 1.000 | 1.163 | 1.540 |
| G_eff / G_N (under the ansatz) | 1.000 | 1.16 | 1.54 |
| (α − β)(z) / (α − β)_0 | 1.000 | 0.93 | 0.81 |

All entries are parameterization-dependent under the effective uniform-ε ansatz of §15.5.1 and are not to be read as Hubble-observable predictions: the mapping to observed H₀ is not derivable from this table alone and requires the full §15.5.2 Hubble-channel pipeline. The (α − β) drift of ~19% across the full cosmic history is structurally modest for a uniform-ε scenario.

**Benchmark A (legacy retention, hardened).** An earlier conservative illustrative point, Benchmark A (ε ≈ 0.010), lies outside the 1σ joint effective band reported above. Benchmark A is retained in the present preprint for historical continuity and as a conservative low-drift illustrative point; it is not representative of the retained-five-probe effective band, and the two benchmarks are not equivalent. Wherever a single-number cosmological benchmark is needed in §15.5 and elsewhere, the working choice is the retained-band midpoint (ε ≈ 0.032), not Benchmark A. Quantitatively, Benchmark A corresponds to the late-time H1-type closure-level Hubble shift ΔH₀/H₀ ≈ 3 % (~2.8 % in the self-consistent numerical solver; see §\ref{sec:mp_hubble} for the detailed closure derivation), i.e. an illustrative late-time benchmark value H₀^late ≈ 69 km/s/Mpc. A code-level robustness check under a mild admissible late-time closure deformation (k_1 = 0.2, λ_3 = 0.1) gives ΔH₀/H₀ ≈ 2.85 % against the benchmark 2.90 %, confirming that Benchmark A is representative of a finite parameter domain rather than a fine-tuned point.
